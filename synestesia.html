GIRL IN THE MIRROR

A picture or reflection means different things to different people. A mirror evokes strong feelings and can be a glance into the future or the past to find the person you once were or wish to become. This is a 3D installation expressing my synesthetic feelings when I look at myself in the mirror.

The roof of the installation depicts the constant, loud thoughts which hit me and make me weaker. The texture created on the inner side of the roof is disturbing, craggy, and rocky and the color depicts the flesh and blood of the brain and also symbolizes its tenderness.

The basic idea behind the installation was that, when I look at myself in the mirror, I feel that everything in the mirror is perfect, calm, and soothing and there is a sense of zeal to reach that place. But at the same time, I am stuck at the place where I am standing, in a mess of emotions, thoughts, and work; and it is difficult or impossible to reach the other side. Even if we are on the colorful and glittery or the gloomy and dark side, we are still going to face societal opinions, expectations which will somehow influence us.

The other side of the mirror depicts the utopian side which has different colors blended on the background. The use of fabric depicts the way I visualize calmness as a slow breeze brushing my skin. The colors yellow, pink, blue symbolizes warmth, optimism, calmness, peace, balance, and growth. The idea behind gold irregular lines on the 3D forms is inspired by the Japanese art form "Kintsugi"("Golden Joinery"). This symbolizes the philosophy of embracing imperfection, highlighting the cracks and repairs as simply an event in the life of the observer, rather than something to breakdown over.
